1. Concept in one paragraph

QuizVerse Tournaments are real-money trivia contests where players pay an entry fee in Brain Coins (BC), play a small pack of questions over a fixed window (daily or weekly), and split a pot. The house seeds every pot, takes a flat 15% rake on top, and pays out the remainder by rank. Three formats ship at launch: Classic (top-N split), Elimination (Survivor-style daily cuts), and Pick-N (PrizePicks-style multipliers). Every parameter — fees, splits, schedules, geo blocks, AMOE rules — lives in a single file, tournament_economy.ts, so a parameter change is one PR.

2. System architecture

Three independently-deployed services, one source of truth (Nakama). Web and Unity clients only ever talk to Nakama RPCs; Bracket is reached through an internal S2S bridge.

Key design decisions

  • Single config file. data/modules/src/p2e/tournament_economy.ts holds the launch slate, rake, splits, AMOE rules, geo blocks, and FX-display rates. Change here = change everywhere.
  • Idempotent entry. Every tournament_enter requires a client-supplied idempotency_key; double-clicks are a single debit.
  • Async-pack model. QuizVerse is async (no real-time match), so each tournament resolves on submitted pack scores within a fixed window.
  • Bracket adapter. The upstream sync match runner (Bracket) is used only for the playoff stage. We adapt sync to async with a 24h round window.
  • Two-stage geo gate. Entry is blocked in WA, AZ, CT, NJ, NY, VA. Redemption is also blocked in CA, NY, VA. AMOE refunds are unlocked by completing 6/6 learning videos.

Classic — top-N pot split

The default format. Players pay an entry fee, the house seeds the pot, and at the end of the window the highest scorers split the (post-rake) pot by rank.

Rank12345678910
Share40%20%10%5%5%5%5%5%2.5%2.5%

Ranks 11-100 share any remaining post-top-10 budget via spreadRemainder() in settlement.ts. Ledger drift target: 0 (sum of payouts + rake must equal the pot).

Elimination — Survivor-style

Open registration for day 0. Each midnight UTC the bottom 50% by accumulated score is eliminated. Survivors at the final cut split the pot equally. #1 of survivors gets a flat final_survivor_bonus_bc (200,000 BC for Wave-1).

For Survivor Week: 5 cuts at 04:00 UTC on Jul 2-6, 2026. ~15,000 entrants → 7,500 → 3,750 → 1,875 → 938 → 469 final survivors.

Pick-N — multiplier slip

Players assemble a "slip" of N picks (Wave-1: N=5) before the lock. The grade unlocks a multiplier:

  • 5/5 → 10x entry returned
  • 4/5 → 5x entry returned
  • 3/5 → 2x entry returned (also the AMOE no-lose threshold)
  • ≤2/5 → 0 (entry stays in pot)

Pick window closes max_pick_window_hours before tournament end (24h for Wave-1). House backstops the pot at up to $100/day if multipliers exceed available pot.

From BC to fiat

BC has a fixed global value (1 USD ≈ 333 BC). What changes is the display: bcToLocalDisplay(bc, country) applies a static FX table to show, e.g., £0.59 in GB or ₹62 in IN for the same 250 BC entry. Display is never used for ledger math — only for marketing and the entry CTA.

6. Tournament lifecycle

A slug travels through 7 statuses, automated by tournament_cron_tick (every 60s) and tournament_settle (on end_iso):

DRAFT → PRE_ENROLL → OPEN → ACTIVE → SETTLING → SETTLED → ARCHIVED

All 11 launch tournaments are at the highlighted PRE_ENROLL stage today. They flip to OPEN at 2026-07-01 04:00 UTC.

stateDiagram-v2 [*] --> DRAFT: seed DRAFT --> PRE_ENROLL: pre_enroll_start_iso PRE_ENROLL --> OPEN: open_start_iso OPEN --> ACTIVE: first submit (or start_iso) ACTIVE --> SETTLING: end_iso SETTLING --> SETTLED: settle cron SETTLED --> ARCHIVED: T+30d
  • DRAFT — not yet visible to clients. Used for ad-hoc admin-created tournaments.
  • PRE_ENROLL — visible, free pre-enroll, no BC charged. Founder cohort cap = 1,000.
  • OPEN — public, BC charged on enter, leaderboard live, push notifications fire on every entry.
  • ACTIVE — same as OPEN; semantic flag once the first pack is submitted.
  • SETTLING — entries closed, cron computing payouts. Typical < 60s.
  • SETTLED — payouts complete; certificates claimable; UI shows results forever.
  • ARCHIVED — hidden from default lists; historical record kept.
