The detailed walkthrough

Meet Sarah. She's 24, lives in Austin, TX, and just heard about QuizVerse from a friend who shared a referral link. She has a phone, a Google account, and zero familiarity with crypto or trivia apps. Today (June 30, 2026) she's going to enter her first tournament. Watch what she sees, what the system does, and what gets stored.
1

Sarah taps her friend's referral link

The link is quizverse.world?ref=AB12CD. The web app loads, mints an anonymous ghost session in 1.5 seconds (device-bound), and stamps the referral code into local storage. Sarah hasn't signed up for anything yet — she's already a user.

2

She lands on /tournaments and sees 11 cards

The page server-side-renders the tournament list (cached 30s). Each card shows the pot, format, and a live "X people pre-enrolled" badge. Sarah filters to "Daily" and taps GK Royale Daily — entry 50 BC, pot 5,000 BC.

3

She pre-enrolls (it's still PRE_ENROLL, public launch is tomorrow)

The CTA on the detail page reads "Pre-enroll free — keep your spot". She taps it. Nakama checks she's user #847 in the founder cohort (cap = 1,000), so she's flagged founder:true and gets the 2x first-win multiplier. Her referrer (the friend) gets +1 attribution point.

4

July 1, 04:00 UTC — the slug flips OPEN

Across all 8 Nakama pods, the tournament_cron_tick cron sees now >= open_start_iso and updates the meta row to status: OPEN. The realtime stream pushes a 1000:opened event to every subscribed client. Sarah gets a push notification: "Tournaments are LIVE. Tap to play."

5

She signs in with Google to keep her wallet across devices

Tap "Sign in with Google" → OAuth round-trip → Nakama merges her ghost user with her Google identity. Her pre-enroll, referral attribution, and any future entries all carry over. The wallet shows 250 BC (a welcome bonus from the signup-rewards service).

6

She taps "Enter" — 50 BC debited

Web client generates an idempotency_key (UUID v4) and calls tournament_enter. Nakama:

  • Validates status (OPEN ✓), country (US ✓), state (TX not blocked ✓), age (24 ✓), rate-limit (1st call ✓)
  • Reads her bc_wallet (250 BC ≥ 50 BC ✓), debits 50 BC
  • Writes a tournament_entries row with paid_via:"balance"
  • Increments tournament_pot.pot_bc by 50 (5,000 → 5,050)
  • Broadcasts a 1001:entry message on the tournament:gk-royale-daily stream

Sarah's UI shows "You're in. Pot is now 5,050 BC." in 1.2 seconds.

7

She fetches today's quiz pack

tournament_content_get_pack returns 5 multiple-choice questions on general knowledge. The correct-answer key stays server-side (anti-cheat); the client only gets the questions and a 60-second time limit per question.

8

She plays the 5 questions

Her score is calculated server-side as correct_count + speed_bonus (faster = more). She gets 4/5 with reasonable latency (avg 850 ms client-server). tournament_submit_pack_result persists the submission and upserts her leaderboard score. The anti-cheat layer flags her as clean (latency > 300 ms floor, daily count < 200 ceiling).

9

She watches the leaderboard tick live

The detail page subscribes to the tournament:gk-royale-daily stream. As other users enter and submit, the pot ticker animates and her rank wobbles between 12 and 18. The stream pushes 1001:entry and 1002:submit events; the UI updates without a single HTTP poll.

10

23:59:59 UTC — settlement begins

The cron sees now >= end_iso and flips status to SETTLING. tournament_settle:

  • Snapshots the leaderboard (final ranking)
  • Computes rake = pot * 0.15 → house
  • Pays the top-10 by share (40/20/10/5/5/5/5/5/2.5/2.5)
  • Refunds AMOE no-lose entrants who finished in top-50%
  • Asserts ledger_drift == 0

Sarah finished rank 14 — outside the top-10 payout — but the founder 2x multiplier applied to her referral points means her friend (the referrer) gets credit for one more attribution.

11

She qualifies for an "I played in the inaugural" badge

Even though she didn't make top-10, every founder-cohort entrant gets a special launch-week certificate. tournament_claim_certificate invokes the cert Lambda, which renders a 1200x630 OG-shareable PNG with her username, rank, and "Founder · GK Royale · Day 1" copy, then uploads to s3://intelli-verse-x-media/certificates/.

12

She shares it on X. Her friend gets a referral payout.

Sarah posts the cert on X. The OG card pre-renders. Her friend was already top-100 in referrers and at T+1h after launch the tournament_referral_settle_topN cron fires Tremendous emails: top-1 gets $500, top-2-3 get $250, top-4-10 get $100, top-11-100 get $25. He gets $25.

What if Sarah lived in NY?

Same flow up to step 6 — except the entry call returns an error.

403 entry_blocked_in_us_state — New York is one of the six states where paid skill-game entries are prohibited (NY, WA, AZ, CT, NJ, VA). The UI immediately shows the AMOE upsell: "Watch 6 short videos to unlock a free entry."
A

She watches the 6-video Learning Series

Each video is ~2 minutes, hosted on CloudFront-cached HLS. learning_video_record_watch records progress. The series is genuinely educational — exam-prep tutorials, GK trivia, mind-sport strategy.

B

She submits her completion check

learning_check_submit verifies 6/6 watched. Nakama writes a tournament_entries row with paid_via:"amoe" and bc_charged:0. Pot stays unchanged (AMOE doesn't add to the pot).

C

She plays exactly the same tournament — no fee

From here on, AMOE entrants are indistinguishable from paid entrants on the leaderboard. If she finishes top-50% she gets the standard payout; if she finishes worse, she gets the AMOE no-lose guarantee — no money was at risk.

This is the legal pillar that keeps QuizVerse out of the gambling bucket in skill-game-prohibited states. Every paid tournament has a free, equivalent path.

Other Day-2 journeys (one-liners)

🎯 Pick-5 Daily

Sarah picks 5 answers in advance, locks her slip 24h before close, and at settlement gets a multiplier — 5/5 = 10x, 4/5 = 5x, 3/5 = 2x, ≤2/5 = 0.

⚔️ Survivor Week

Sarah enters Day 0 along with ~15,000 others. Each midnight, the bottom 50% are eliminated. If she survives all 5 cuts she splits the pot equally with ~469 other survivors.

🥇 Bracket Playoff

The top-64 of Brain Bowl Weekly feed into a 6-round single-elimination bracket. Each round = a 24h pack-submission window; highest score wins. Sarah didn't qualify but watches her friend's bracket via the iframe.

📚 Learning Series

For the AP 2027 Prep Sprint, the Learning Series doubles as test prep. Sarah completes 6 videos (a real benefit) and unlocks a free AMOE entry into the $25k-pot tournament.

💸 Buy-BC sheet

Sarah's wallet runs low. She taps "Top up", picks the $5 / 1,500 BC pack, pays via Apple Pay or Stripe, and is back in within 8 seconds.

💰 Redeem to fiat

Sarah accumulates 50,000 BC over a month. She taps "Redeem", picks Tremendous, and converts to a $150 Visa eGift card (geo-blocked in CA, NY, VA — see REDEMPTION_BLOCK_US_STATES).
