How it works

One click in the LMS. Zero friction for students.

We implement the same open standard trusted by universities worldwide — LTI 1.3 with the full LTI Advantage suite — so the whole loop is automatic.

1

Teacher picks a quiz

Through Deep Linking, the teacher browses QuizVerse packs (or imports their own Canvas/Moodle quiz) and drops it into the course. A graded assignment and gradebook column are created in one step.

2

Student clicks & plays

The LTI launch signs the student in instantly — no account creation, no password. They get a fast, modern quiz player with autosave, explanations, and progress tracking.

3

Grade lands in the LMS

The moment the attempt is submitted, we post the score to the LMS via Assignment & Grade Services. It appears in the Canvas or Moodle gradebook automatically — no CSVs, no re-typing.

The standards, explained

Built on the open standards your LMS already speaks

No proprietary lock-in. QuizVerse is built on the same two open 1EdTech standards that power the modern edtech ecosystem — here's what they mean for your school, in plain language.

LTI · Learning Tools Interoperability

The plug that connects tools to your LMS

LTI is the 1EdTech (formerly IMS Global) standard that lets external tools like QuizVerse plug directly into Canvas and Moodle. It handles single sign-on — students click once in the course and they're in — plus secure sharing of the user and course data the tool needs, and automatic grade sync back to the LMS gradebook. The current generation, LTI 1.3 with the LTI Advantage services, secures every exchange with OAuth 2.0 and signed JWT tokens, so no passwords ever leave the LMS.

Canvas LMS moodle
QTI · Question & Test Interoperability

The shipping container for quizzes

QTI is the industry standard for packaging and sharing assessments across systems. Instead of retyping questions when you change platforms, a QTI package carries the whole quiz — question text, answer choices, correct answers, points, and metadata — in a format both sides understand. Export a quiz from Moodle and import it into Canvas, or the other way around, with question formats and metadata preserved. QuizVerse uses QTI (alongside Moodle XML) to move your quizzes in and out without loss.

FAQ

Questions schools ask us

Do students need a QuizVerse account?

No. The LTI 1.3 launch from Canvas or Moodle authenticates the student automatically and provisions a linked identity behind the scenes. There is no signup screen, no password, and no token to copy.

How do grades get back into our gradebook?

Through LTI Assignment & Grade Services — the same official mechanism used by major publishers. When a student submits, QuizVerse posts the score to the assignment's line item. If the LMS is briefly unreachable, we queue and retry until it lands, and teachers can trigger a manual re-sync anytime.

Which quiz formats can you import?

Canvas QTI exports (including whole-course Common Cartridge packages) and Moodle XML question-bank exports, with images preserved. Multiple choice, true/false and multi-select convert today; matching, numerical and short answer are on the roadmap.

Which LMS versions are supported?

Canvas (Instructure-hosted or self-hosted) with LTI 1.3 enabled, and Moodle 4.5 LTS through 5.x — every currently supported Moodle release ships full LTI Advantage platform support.

What about privacy and compliance?

We request the minimum launch data needed to run the quiz and sync the grade. Student data is encrypted at rest, never sold, and handled in line with FERPA. Parental-consent gating is built in for under-13 contexts.

Are you 1EdTech certified?

Not yet — and we won't claim it until we are. QuizVerse is built on the open 1EdTech standards (LTI 1.3, LTI Advantage, QTI), and our roadmap targets LTI Advantage Complete certification through the official 1EdTech conformance suite, followed by TrustEd Apps vetting. Pilot schools get full visibility into our conformance test results along the way.

Can we move quizzes between Canvas and Moodle?

Yes — that's a free by-product of the bridge. Import from one, export to the other. QuizVerse converts between Canvas QTI and Moodle XML in both directions.
