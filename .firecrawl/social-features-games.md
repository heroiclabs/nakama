# Social Features in Mobile Games: What Players Expect in 2025

From leaderboards to guilds, what are the most popular social features in mobile games that players engage most with in 2025?

![](https://maf.ad/wp-content/uploads/2023/05/MAF_pictogram_150-96x96.png)

Can you name a popular mobile game with zero social features?

Tough task, right?

That’s because social features in mobile games have gone from being just an option to a **must-have strategy for boosting player engagement**.

There is a **wide range of social features** you can include in your game, depending on the genre, player preferences, and your goals. Let’s discuss their importance and explore all the different forms they can take.

## **Growing Importance of Social Features in Mobile Games**

Back in the day, social features were almost exclusive to mid-core games such as shooters and strategy games.

However, over time, these features **became standard across genres—from casual to puzzle games**. While having a guild or PvP feature in a [casual game](https://maf.ad/glossary/casual-games/) would have been quite weird 7 years ago, today, it’s a completely normal thing.

According to data.ai, today, ⅔ of top mobile games have at least one social feature.

What changed?

Nowadays, **players expect connection and community features in games**, no matter what type of game they are playing. The pandemic also played a role here, as many people turned to games for social interaction during isolation periods. Moreover, with the rise of UA costs, developers are increasingly focused on engaging and retaining players.

### **Impact and Player Preferences**

Social features in mobile games are a powerful tool for boosting player retention, monetization, and [community building](https://maf.ad/en/blog/mobile-game-communities/).

Being social in a game can give players **extra reasons to stay active for longer**. While some players simply enjoy being a part of a group, others are motivated by competing with others.

Just like in everyday life, there are players who are more and less social.

Some players are true social butterflies.

These are [player personas](https://maf.ad/en/player-personas-mobile-games/) who **consider social features essential to their gaming experience** and seek games that allow them to communicate, team up, or play against others. They tend to enjoy genres like MOBA and MMORPG games with features like clans, guilds, teams, PvP modes, chat, etc.

On the other hand, some players **enjoy occasional social interactions** without the pressure of constant engagement. Such players often prefer genres like puzzle and casual strategy games.

## **Common Social Features in Mobile Games**

Which social features can you include in your game?

Here are some of your options—let’s go over the **most common and trending social features in mobile games**.

### **Social Media Integration**

![social features in mobile games 8 ball pool social media integration](https://maf.ad/wp-content/uploads/2025/01/8-ball-pool-social-media-integration-1024x474.png)Social media integration in 8 Ball Pool

Whether you install a [simulation](https://maf.ad/en/blog/simulation-mobile-games-market/), sports, strategy game or else—you’ll probably find a social media integration feature.

By **logging in with social media** (e.g., Google or Facebook), players unlock a set of social features such as sharing achievements and inviting friends to the game.

Oftentimes, players need a little push to do this.

To encourage social media connections, games often **reward players with a generous amount of currency** for linking their accounts. After all, people put a great deal of trust in friend recommendations, creating a powerful strategy for organic growth.

### **Chat**

Don’t want players leaving your game to communicate? Give them an in-game chat platform.

Chat features are particularly popular in [mid-core games](https://maf.ad/en/blog/mid-core-games/) like shooters, RPGs, and strategy games **where players need to coordinate and strategize** in real-time.

The most common form of in-game chat is **text chat**, where players can send text messages, emojis, but also premade phrases for faster communication. In certain genres like MOBAs and multiplayer RPGs, it’s also common to have **voice chats** for direct communication, which allows players to react instantly.

For example, the mobile version of the popular shooter game Call of Duty allows players to communicate with teammates during matches via both voice and text chat.

### **Leaderboards**

![social features in mobile games leaderboards subway surfers](https://maf.ad/wp-content/uploads/2025/01/subway-surfers-leaderboards.png)Country-wide and friend list leaderboards in Subway Surfers

The leaderboard is one of the most popular social features in mobile games, and you can find it in almost every genre.

Leaderboards rank players based on their performance compared to others. For example, players can check how they stand against the **rest of the world, their country, or people from their friends list**.

This feature is particularly effective among competitive players, motivating them to perform better, climb the ladder, and get the recognition they strive for.

### **Co-Op and PvP Modes**

Does your game attract players who like to play in teams or compete?

Then consider adding a co-op mode to engage players who prefer to **work together towards a common goal**. For example, complete missions or defeat bosses.

However, If you think of your players as a more competitive crowd, think about adding a **PvP (player vs. player) mode**.

![pvp mode harry potter magic awakened](https://maf.ad/wp-content/uploads/2025/01/pvp-modes-harry-potter-magic-awakened.png)Different PvP features in Harry Potter: Magic Awakened

There are two types of PvP play—synchronous (competing in real-time) and asynchronous (turn-based play).

If the US is one of your go-to markets, note that real-time PvP is particularly popular there. According to GameRefinery, **32% to 34% of the top mobile games in the US have a synchronous PvP mode**.

PvP is commonly found in action, MOBA, and strategy games, boosting engagement and creating **additional monetization opportunities**. Think about it—competitive players are often willing to invest in upgrades, boosts, or cosmetics to gain an advantage or show off in front of others.

### **Friend Systems**

Playing with friends can be a great motivation to play a game for a long time.

There are different friend systems you can include in your game, and some popular options include:

- **Activity feeds:** showing updates on what friends are doing in the game, such as high scores, milestones and completed challenges.
- **Ask/send help:** when stuck in the game, players can request or send assistance like extra lives or resources from their friends.
- **Trading and gifting**: allowing players to exchange in-game items or send gifts to friends.
- **Friend challenges:** giving players the option to directly challenge friends to matches, high-score battles, etc.
- **Spectator mode:** some mid-core games allow players to watch their friends play in real-time from their perspective.

![candy cruash saga ask for lives](https://maf.ad/wp-content/uploads/2025/01/ask-lives-social-feature-candy-crush.png)“Ask for lives” feature in Candy Crush Saga

### **Guilds/Clans/Teams**

If there were a popularity contest for social features in mobile games, guilds would be a guaranteed winner.

To define, guilds are **groups or teams that players can join within a game**, allowing them to collaborate, socialize, and compete.

Some other expressions for guilds are clans and teams—the terms vary from game to game, but the principle is the same.

Guilds have seen **tremendous popularity in the last few years**, evolving from a mid-core staple to a widely adopted feature across game genres.

Some popular games that include guilds are, for example, mid-core hits like Clash of Clans and [AFK Journey](https://maf.ad/en/blog/afk-journey-analysis/), as well as casual hits like Coin Master and Candy Crush Saga.

Finding examples of guilds in games is quite easy. Why? Well, this feature is very widespread. According to GameRefinery, about **70% of the top-grossing games in the US have this feature**.

![social features in mobile games guilds teams coin master](https://maf.ad/wp-content/uploads/2025/01/coin-master-guild-team.png)Guild (team) feature in Coin Master

### **Social-Based Events and Social Currency**

Want to add a bit of social spirit to your live events strategy?

Consider crafting **time-limited events based on social interactions**. For example, group missions to defeat a boss or reach a certain goal, or compete in team-based competitions before the event ends.

Such events can be very exciting for players, boosting engagement and retention. By making these events temporary, you also build anticipation, encouraging players to log in regularly and participate.

![candy crush soda saga social event](https://maf.ad/wp-content/uploads/2025/01/candy-crush-soda-saga-social-event-1024x445.png)Candy Crush Soda Saga time-limited social event “Bake a Cake”

Many social events come with their own **unique reward system—social currency**. Different from regular currency, players can earn social currency by engaging in activities like time-limited social events, guilds, or competing in PvP modes.

Social currency is particularly popular in RPG games, found in 88% of the top RPG apps (Statista).

### **Social Push Notifications**

Sometimes, players need a little reminder to participate in social activities.

With [push notifications](https://maf.ad/en/blog/best-practices-for-push-notifications/), you can **notify players about different social matters**. For example, about chat messages, gifts received from friends, that it’s their turn to play, reminders that their friends are still playing, etc.

However, moderation is key.

Sending too many notifications can be overwhelming and annoying for players. To maximize their effectiveness, **segment players based on their preferences and behavior**. For example, by sending occasional reminders to casual players and more frequent updates to highly engaged players.

## **Closing Words on Social Features in Mobile Games**

That’s it—these are the key things to know about social features, why they matter, how they shape player engagement, and what their most common types are.

As mobile games continue to evolve, social elements will only become more important. As a developer, the key is to find the right mix that fits your game and keeps players coming back.
